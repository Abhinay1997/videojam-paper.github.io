VideoJAM: Joint Appearance-Motion Representations for Enhanced Motion Understanding in Video Models

Bla1 Bla2 Bla3
Bla AI
Paper arXiv Benchmark

Abstract

Despite tremendous recent progress, generative video models continue to struggle with fundamental aspects of real-world dynamics, motion and physics. We show that this limitation can be largely attributed to the conventional pixel reconstruction objective, which biases the model toward appearance fidelity at the expense of temporal coherence. To address this, we introduce VideoJAM, a novel framework to instill an effective motion prior to video generators, by encouraging the model to learn joint appearance-motion representations. VideoJAM is constructed of two complementary units. During training, we extend the objective to predict both the generated pixels and their corresponding motion, derived from a single learned representation. During inference, we introduce Inner-Guidance, a mechanism that steers the model toward temporally coherent generations by leveraging its own evolving motion predictions for guidance. Notably, our framework can be applied to any video model with minimal adaptations, and requires no additional data nor scaling of the model. VideoJAM sets state-of-the-art results in motion modeling, surpassing highly competitive proprietary models while also enhancing the perceived quality of the generations, suggesting that the two axes, appearance and motion, can and should be complementary

Motion Incoherence in Video Generation

State-of-the-art video generators struggle to model real-world motion and physics. We identify four categories that current models struggle with: (a) basic motion (e.g., walking, running), (b) complex motion (e.g., gymnastics), (c) physics (e.g., solid objects that move through other objects), and (d) rotational motion (e.g., spinning). Below are examples of failure cases from each category obtained with a DiT-30B video generation model.

(a) Basic motion: A close-up of a jogger's feet as they run along a rocky coastal path
(b) Complex motion: A young man performing a cartwheel on a gray surface
(c) Physics: A woman enjoying the fun of hula hooping
(d) Rotational motion: A hand holding a yellow fidget spinner
