VideoJAM: Joint Appearance-Motion Representations for Enhanced Motion Generation in Video Models

Paper ID #1047

Supplementary Material

Abstract
Despite tremendous recent progress, generative video models continue to struggle with fundamental aspects of real-world dynamics and physics. We identify the conventional pixel reconstruction objective as a key factor causing models to favor appearance at the expense of temporal coherence. To address this, we introduce VideoJAM, a novel framework to instill an effective motion prior to video generators, by encouraging the model to learn a joint appearance-motion representation. VideoJAM is constructed of two complementary units. During training, we extend the objective to predict both the generated pixels and their corresponding motion, derived from a single learned representation. During inference, we introduce Inner-Guidance, a mechanism that steers the generation toward coherent motion by leveraging the model's own evolving motion predictions as a dynamic guidance signal. Notably, our framework can be applied to any video model with minimal adaptations, requiring no additional data or scaling of the model. VideoJAM sets state-of-the-art results in motion generation, surpassing highly competitive proprietary models while also enhancing the perceived visual quality of the generations. These findings emphasize that appearance and motion can be complementary and, when integrated, enhance both video realism and coherence.

Motion Incoherence in Video Generation

State-of-the-art video generators struggle to model real-world dynamics and physics. We identify four categories that current models struggle with: (a) basic motion (e.g., walking, running), (b) complex motion (e.g., gymnastics), (c) physics (e.g., solid objects that move through other objects), and (d) rotational motion (e.g., spinning).

(c) Physics: A woman enjoying the fun of hula hooping
(d) Rotational motion: A hand holding a yellow fidget spinner
